Leads Scoring Case Study

X Education is an online education company aiming to boost its lead conversion rate, which currently stands at around 30%. The company attracts potential customers through various marketing channels, including online platforms and search engines. Visitors to the website can explore courses, fill out forms, or engage with content, with those providing contact details classified as leads. To improve efficiency in converting these leads into paying customers, X Education seeks to identify 'Hot Leads'—leads with a high likelihood of conversion. This approach is expected to enhance the sales team's focus on the most promising prospects, ultimately aiming to achieve a target conversion rate of 80%.

To address this challenge, we have developed a machine learning model using Logistic Regression and Random Forest algorithms. The process involved comprehensive feature engineering and exploratory data analysis (EDA) to identify key factors influencing lead conversion. The model assigns a lead score to each prospect, indicating their likelihood of becoming a customer. By prioritizing leads with higher scores, the company can optimize its sales efforts, significantly improving the overall conversion rate.
